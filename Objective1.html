Minimize implementation cost: Initial capital investment is a critical component of a cost-benefit analysis which is frequently used by dam owners to choose among different management alternatives (Whitelaw & Macmullan 2002). Implementation cost includes the cost of removing or repairing the dam and appurtenant structures, permitting costs, and mitigation costs (Walls and Shabman 2020). Decisions to retrofit, repair, and remove dams all incur implementation costs.

1b. Minimize maintenance cost: Ongoing capital investment required to support the management decision is also a critical component of cost-benefit analysis.
