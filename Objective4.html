4a. Maximize swift water recreational opportunities: Dam removal can increase the recreational use of the river and surrounding landscape, particularly for swift-water based recreation like rafting and tubing (Loomis et al. 2002, Leisher et al. 2021)

4b. Maximize flat water recreational opportunities: Recreation is the most common primary purpose of dams in the National Inventory of Dams, accounting for 32% of primary purposes among all structures; however, dams that no longer serve their intended purpose are frequently assigned recreation as their primary purpose in the NID (Gonzales & Walls 2020).

4c. Minimize adverse impacts to sites judged by the community, state, or Nation to be historically significant places: Historically significant sites are frequently subject to conservation measures under the National Historic Preservation Act and state and tribal equivalent laws (McClain et al 2008). Even sites without formal recognition may be important to a community’s historic memory (Fox et al. 2016).

4d. Minimize interruption to community sense of place: Opposition to dam removals is often fomented by the entangling of “regional identity, history, and personal experience” that make dams become symbols of community identity (Fox et al. 2016).
