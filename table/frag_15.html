Connectivity methods

Numerous connectivity methods are available to describe potential changes in species access to a river network with barrier addition or removal (see McKay et al. 2017, Jumani et al. 2020)

Note that flow intermittency may be an important consideration in calculating connectivity, particularly in dryland rivers (Marshall et al. 2021)

Key distinctions among methods:

  • Passability of barriers parameterized as binary vs. continuous
  • All reaches considered suitable habitat vs.suitable reaches determined by a model or historic data
  • Spatial relationship among habitats required life cycle completion (e.g., metrics for anadromous organisms quantify connectivity to the ocean while metrics for resident or potamodromous organisms may not)

Data requirements/sources

Geospatial model of the river network (e.g., NHDPlus), passability of barriers (see Kemp & O’Hanley 2010), and suitable habitat within the network
