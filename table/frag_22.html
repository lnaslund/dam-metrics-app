Estimated landscape greenhouse gas balance

Landscape greenhouse gas balance (here defined as greenhouse gas balance from the reservoir impounded area) is generally estimated using measurements from similar systems. Most estimates of reservoir greenhouse gas emissions quantify CO2 and CH4 emissions only, as nitrous oxide (N2O) is responsible for an average of only 4% of the radiative forcing from reservoirs (Deemer et al. 2016). Tools like g-res are useful for estimating greenhouse gas emissions attributable to the reservoir while it is impounded. To estimate the change in landscape greenhouse gas balance following dam removal, many other gas sources and sinks in the reservoir footprint should be accounted for including: soil CO2 emissions, river CO2 emissions, tree CO2 uptake, soil CH4 uptake, river CH4 emissions, tree CH4 emissions, as well as the transitional emissions from decomposition of newly exposed organic matter in the former reservoir sediments.

For hydropower dams, carbon intensity–CO2-eq emissions per unit electricity generated–may be an appropriate method important for evaluating the environmental impact of hydropower generation at a particular dam.

Data requirements/sources

Data concerning characteristics of the reservoir relevant for estimating greenhouse gas emissions (e.g., trophic status), relevant gas balance estimates from similar systems, component emissions/uptake estimates from similar systems
