Connectivity methods

Connectivity methods are similar in form to single organism metrics decribed in objective 5a.

Variations include metrics which:

  • Account for the hydrologic complexity of unfragmented portions of the river network (e.g., # of Strahler orders represented)
  • Prioritize connectivity among sites with high habitat assessment scores

Note that flow intermittency may be an important consideration in calculating connectivity, particularly in dryland rivers (Marshall et al. 2021).

Data requirements/sources

Geospatial model of the river network (e.g., NHDPlus), including the Strahler order attribute; habitat scores of reaches
